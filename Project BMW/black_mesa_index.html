Who we are?

Founded in 1950 as a state-owned company with heavy focus on science development, Black Mesa now highlights itself as the main protagonist of the national science division. Although our main research facilities are located in North America, we have expanded our funding campaigns to numerous countries and promoted partnerships and projects with many well-recognized organizations around the world. All in the name of science and cooperation between the United States and the rest of the world, maintaining national interests while spreading development across the globe.

Aside, an image of the 2019's "Space-BM" convention, helded in New Zealand in cooperation with Rocket Lab, a company specialized in creating heavy propulsion rockets. Working together with a common goal, we've managed to further improve humanity's grasp on space.

What is our mission?

Safe Science

Serve the People

In our labs, we conduct both pure research and applied science. Due to the potential danger that comes when dealing with hazardous materials and potential life-threating experiments, precaution is our number one priority. All Black Mesa research facilities are designed and constructed with evacuation plans, safety protocols and accident prevention in mind. The Black Mesa personel are trained in disaster-response and instructed to follow every single safety rule in the complex. We can never be too careful, but that doesn't stop us from having the most high-security research facilities in the entire country, helping us to minimize any possible danger.

We do science for one reason: serving the nation and mankind. Black Mesa plays a vital role in the country's development by providing America with critical tecnology to defend our country, save lives, supporting inovation and improve the life of all citizens. To assist in our goals, we colaborate not only with the government but also with many institutions and organizations.
